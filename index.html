What is APRILVIS?

The next generation of sound evaluation

Have you always wondered how pleasant or eventful the sounds in your city are? APRILVIS can tell you just that! Our system goes beyond measuring decibels; it measures what people actually think of sounds, via something that is known as their appraisal of the sounds. This appraisal is based on two dimensions: pleasantness and eventfulness. APRILVIS is able to automatically generate and visualize these appraisals and classify them as lively, calm, boring or chaotic.

In just three simple steps APRILVIS can give you information about the sonic environment in your area.

  1. Upload a soundfile of at least 20 minutes.
  2. Let the system do its magic.
  3. Interpret the results.

Application

It has been established that noise annoyance can have a big influence on a person’s quality of life. Research has shown that it can have serious effects on a person's health, and it can hinder people in their day-to-day life. At the moment noise annoyance is usually talked about in terms of decibels. However, high decibel levels do not necessarily have to be annoying and detrimental to health. Sometimes we appreciate loud sounds, like our favourite music or the sound of playing children. Research has shown that measuring decibels does not account for personal differences and the appraisal of sounds.

APRILVIS is able to appraise sounds like an average person would do it, to get a better understanding of the quality of these sounds. These automatically generated appraisals could be used to gain more insight in the qualitative information of sonic environments at certain places. Local governments and authorities could use this information to map the sound quality in certain areas, to find out if it’s necessary to reduce or eliminate annoying sound sources.

About APRILVIS

Appraisal of Recordings In urban Locations:
the Visualisation of Soundscapes

APRILVIS was developed during the Sound Recognition course at the University of Groningen. It all started with ten students recorded sounds in the city of Assen. We personally experienced that some soundscapes were annoying while others were not. This inspired us to develop a system that is able to give qualitative information about a recording. Our motto: “We need a system that works.”

The name stands for: Appraisal of Recording In urban Locations: the Visualisation of Soundscapes. We focused on visualizing urban soundscapes based on how humans appraise sound recordings. We categorized the appraisals of these soundscapes as lively, calm, boring or chaotic. Human appraisals are "cognitive evaluations of events that are considered to be the proximal psychological determinants of emotional experience, with different combinations of appraisals corresponding to different emotions".
(Source: P. Kuppens, D. Champagne, and F. Tuerlinckx, “The dynamic interplay between appraisal and core affect in daily life,” Frontiers in Psychology, vol. 3, pp. 1–8, 2012.)
